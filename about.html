About Me + My Progress

Like I said before, I'm a 20 year old struggling student, doing my best, keeping my cool, still being a nerd watching anime and playing my visual novels and whatnot. I'm getting more into art than I was before (I gave up for a bit), I even bought a drawing tablet. I barely had time to use it though, but maybe I will now with my short winter break. Taking a web design class has a ride. When I first started, I barely ahd any idea what I was doing, I hardly remembered anything I thought I learned before. I barely even knew the difference between HTML and CSS, like, HTML is the structure, and CSS is the style, right? But luckily it didn't take long for me to figure it out. I got the hang of most things pretty quickly I think, well at least in the beginning. Really, my main struggle was understanding what certain things were meant for, like using sticky vs fixed vs absolute and things like that. Some things are easy though, like the favicon part, which was mostly copying and pasting. Weirdly that was my favorite part (lol), I remember when I saw the favicon on my iphone thinking it was really cool. Seeing the bigger picture it all eventually started to make sense. I got familiar with VSCode and its plugins and shortcuts which have been helpful, especially for catching little mistakes and formatting things neatly. I also learned a lot about basic CSS styles, like how to change fonts, colors, and align things properly on the page. Figuring out layouts was one of the harder parts, especially when it came to positioning things like images and using floats, margins, and padding. To keep everything straight, I started taking notes on the structure of a page and the elements and stuff, thank goodness I did because it really helped me stay organized and memorize how everything fits together, rules and things of that sort. Now that I’m more familiar with HTML and CSS, I might consider learning more and turning website-making into a side hustle. I’m not sure I’d want to make it my whole career, though, it’s fun, but it’s definitely a lot of work, and it did burn me out at times. But honestly it did inspire me to explore other computer related work. :]
